About Daniel

I am taking a gap year between sixth form and university. While I can't travel for obvious reasons, I've decided to work on a passion project that will showcase one of my favourite hobbies. I am not a real bartender by any stretch of the imagination, and I am certainly not a trained programmer, but I enjoy both on an amateur level and so am combining both of them on this website.

About Cocktails

I love cocktails! I love drinking them and I love making them for myself and others even more. I think they are a great way to connect with people and to have a good time. They create intrigue and interest for the people drinking them and a feeling of satisfaction for the person making them, it's great when I can see people are enjoying themselves.

When I started drinking I never really got along with beer and ales that well because, let's face it, they’re not for everyone. I wanted something that I would really enjoy, so I started on cider. Yeah it was alright but I wasn’t quite there yet. So then one day at a friends house I got a bottle of Absolut Apple, now we’re going places. I liked it and having naively entered the land of spirits I tried other things until I stumbled upon my favourite spirit: gin. I brought a bottle of it to another friend's party and drank some of it straight like a madman (which I wouldn’t recommend) and then had the rest another time with some lemonade.

I continued to dabble in alcohol until things kicked off about a year or two later. I can’t pinpoint what it was that made me want to start making cocktails, whether I was just bored or I saw some stuff online, I can’t remember. But one day I made my mind up that I would give it a shot and see if it was fun so I bought a big bottle of gin and had a look at some recipes. On the 25th of september 2019 I launched my cocktail instagram as a handy place to store my recipes and I started it off with a Negroni. Now I know that wasn’t the best cocktail to get started with as it is very bitter and potent and hence I wasn’t huge on it the first time round. They say in Italy it takes three times to get to like them and I think they’re right. Nonetheless, I was committed and continued with really awesome cocktails like the Bee’s Knees and the Tom Collins (along with some weird ones like the cucumber lemonade).

From the start I had been making cocktails just for myself and my dad, but when I started making them for friends and having cocktail nights I started to realise the other reason why I enjoy it so much is that it is exciting to see them being made and very intriguing for the person that has never tasted it before.

So that leads us to where we are now. I’m still a novice who doesn’t really know what they are doing, but I do have a lot more bottles in the cabinet than I had at the start.
